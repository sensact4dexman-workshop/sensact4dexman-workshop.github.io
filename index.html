About the workshop

Dexterous robotic manipulation remains one of the most demanding frontiers in robotics, requiring tight coordination between algorithms, sensing, actuation, control, and system-level integration. Recent advances in sensing and actuation technologies, from high-resolution tactile arrays to energy-dense actuators, have expanded the physical capabilities of robotic manipulation systems. However, advances at the component level often progress independently of task-level manipulation requirements, leading to a disconnect between device capabilities and real-world demands.

This workshop will bring together academic and industry leaders working on materials, sensing, actuation, haptics, and robotic systems to explore how emerging hardware technologies can better enable robust, high-performance dexterous manipulation. Through keynote talks, panel discussions, lightning talks, and interactive demo and poster sessions, the workshop aims to foster cross-disciplinary dialogue and long-term collaboration between researchers developing physical technologies and those advancing robotic manipulation algorithms and systems.

Topics of interest include:

  • Tactile and multimodal sensing for robotic perception
  • Distributed and conformable sensing surfaces
  • Compliant and high-performance actuation technologies
  • Haptic devices, interfaces, and rendering
  • Teleoperation and human–robot interaction
  • Hardware platforms for data collection and human demonstrations
  • Tactile perception and interpretation
  • Learning-based dexterous manipulation
  • Simulation and sim-to-real transfer for manipulation hardware
  • Co-design of sensing, actuation, and robotic systems
  • Whole-body manipulation
